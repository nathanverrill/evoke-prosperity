## EVOKE Prosperity: Narrative Framing & Transmedia Experience

One of the biggest design decisions we've made is that **EVOKE Prosperity is not simply an LMS course with some game elements.** It is a **transmedia experience** where every platform serves a different role in the story.

The learner isn't told they are "taking a class."

They're invited to become an **EVOKE Agent** participating in an unfolding mission.

---

# The Core Framing

The experience exists simultaneously on three levels.

## 1. The real world

The student lives in the real world.

They interview people.
They build projects.
They upload evidence.
They collaborate with classmates.

This is where actual learning happens.

---

## 2. The EVOKE Operations Hub

The web application is not an LMS.

It is presented as the operational interface used by EVOKE agents.

Think:

- mission control
- operations center
- intelligence portal
- dossier archive

Instead of:

- Assignments
- Grades
- Discussion Boards

the learner sees:

- Missions
- Intelligence
- Evidence
- Operations
- Rewards
- Team Progress

This framing keeps the learner inside the fiction while still completing authentic educational work.

---

## 3. The Basin Simulation (Minecraft)

Minecraft is **not "the game."**

Minecraft is **the simulation.**

That distinction is important.

The student is never told:

> Go play Minecraft.

Instead they're told:

> Access the Basin Simulation.

The simulation allows agents to:

- investigate locations
- observe infrastructure
- inspect environmental conditions
- discover clues
- meet characters
- understand systems that are difficult to explain with text alone

Minecraft is effectively a **living digital twin** of the fictional world.

---

# Why Minecraft Exists

Minecraft is used because some concepts are better experienced than explained.

Instead of reading:

> Water is diverted here...

students physically follow canals.

Instead of reading:

> The mine damaged the ecosystem...

they walk through it.

Instead of hearing about Oasis...

they eventually arrive there.

The simulation builds intuition.

It makes systems visible.

---

# What Students Actually Do

Students never "beat Minecraft."

Minecraft is exploratory.

Typical objectives include:

- Follow the flow.
- Visit Keel.
- Reach Halyard.
- Discover Oasis.
- Observe changes.
- Ask questions.
- Look for evidence.
- Form hypotheses.

Afterwards...

they return to the Operations Hub and submit evidence.

Minecraft informs learning.

The LMS evaluates learning.

---

# B1llbot's Role

B1llbot is **not the narrator.**

He is not the teacher.

He is not the quest giver.

He is not ChatGPT inside Minecraft.

He is a **field guide.**

Think of him as:

- local businessman
- mentor
- tour guide
- observer
- friendly expert

He knows the Basin because he has lived there.

---

## Personality

We intentionally made him:

- calm
- patient
- practical
- optimistic
- slightly humorous
- approachable

He talks like someone successful who enjoys helping newcomers.

Not an AI.

Not a robot.

Not a wizard.

Just B1llbot.

---

## Why a Businessman?

This was deliberate.

EVOKE Prosperity is ultimately about:

- entrepreneurship
- ownership
- solving problems
- opportunity
- creating value

B1llbot naturally frames problems this way.

Rather than saying:

> This is terrible.

He asks:

> What opportunity do you see here?

---

## What B1llbot Does

He answers questions.

He points learners toward interesting locations.

He asks reflective questions.

He encourages curiosity.

He never gives away every answer.

Examples:

> "What do you notice about the water?"

> "Why do you think this community developed here?"

> "Have you compared Keel and Oasis?"

His goal is observation.

Not lectures.

# Teams

Students may work individually or in teams.

Teams represent **EVOKE field teams.**

A team can:

- complete missions
- submit evidence
- unlock accomplishments
- earn badges
- compare progress

This makes collaboration part of the fiction instead of simply "group work."

---

# Why the Fiction Matters

One important design principle we've discussed repeatedly is that **students are never pretending to do meaningful work—they are actually doing meaningful work inside a compelling narrative.**

The story exists to answer:

> Why does this assignment matter?

Instead of:

> Write a reflection.

The learner hears:

> Intelligence from the Basin is incomplete. We need your observations.

Instead of:

> Upload your assignment.

They see:

> Submit Mission Evidence.

The task is identical.

The motivation changes.

---

# The Long-Term Story

Across the entire experience, learners gradually uncover the history of the Basin:

- Phase 1 — Invisible Asset Trap
- Phase 2 — Corporate Expansion
- Phase 3 — Collapse
- Phase 4 — Property Reclamation

Rather than delivering this as a lecture, learners discover it through:

- graphic novel chapters
- environmental storytelling
- conversations with B1llbot
- mission dossiers
- documents
- observations in the simulation
- instructor feedback

Each mission reveals another piece of the world.

---

# Guiding Philosophy

Everything in EVOKE Prosperity supports one simple illusion:

> **The learner is not completing school assignments. They are an EVOKE Agent investigating a real problem inside a living world.**

The graphic novel creates emotional investment.

The Operations Hub organizes the mission.

The real world provides authentic action.

Minecraft serves as the Basin simulation where learners observe complex systems firsthand.

B1llbot acts as a trusted local guide who encourages curiosity rather than providing answers.

When learners return from the simulation, they submit evidence, receive feedback from AI and human mentors, earn recognition, and unlock the next chapter of the story—creating a continuous loop where narrative, gameplay, and authentic project-based learning reinforce one another.
