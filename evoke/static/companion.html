The Rule of Law in Keel

Keel wasn't rebuilt by accident — it runs on a charter every visitor agrees to, posted at the gates for as long as anyone can remember:

  1. Every person you meet is real. Treat them the way you'd want to be treated — no bullying, no harassment, no cruelty. The Basin has no room for it.
  2. Keep your real name, address, phone number, and school to yourself. In the Basin, your Agent handle is who you are.
  3. Bring a grown-up with you, in spirit. A parent, guardian, or teacher should know you're here — this world is meant to be shared, not sneaked into.
  4. If something feels wrong, leave and say something. Tell a trusted adult right away. The Basin stays safe because people speak up.
  5. The Watchtower keeps a record. What happens in the Basin is logged, same as any shared space — for safety, not surveillance.
