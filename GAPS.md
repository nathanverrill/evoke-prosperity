# GAPS.md — what this build is still missing

An honest audit of the gamified transmedia experience as specified in [`BUILD_PLAN.md`](BUILD_PLAN.md) + [`UI_SPEC.md`](UI_SPEC.md) + [`GAME_DESIGN.md`](GAME_DESIGN.md), against the ambition in `docs/canon/` and the Urgent Evoke lineage. Five lenses: technical, narrative, UX, gamification, business & adoption. Each item is tagged **[decision]** (needs Nathan/stakeholder call before anyone builds) or **[build]** (scoped work), with the ones that can sink the pilot called out first.

---

## The five that matter most

1. **~~Minecraft Java vs. Bedrock~~ [RESOLVED → build risk]** — decided: the server is Java (Fabric) modified to accept Bedrock clients via **Geyser + Floodgate**, per `BUILD_PLAN.md`. The residual risks to engineer around: Floodgate players have prefixed usernames and non-Mojang UUIDs (the reward bridge's username matching and the account-linking flow must handle both identity shapes); Geyser is version-locked to specific Minecraft/Fabric builds (pin everything, upgrade deliberately); and some Java-side visuals/interactions degrade on Bedrock clients — test the actual quest locations in `basin` from a Bedrock device, not just a Java client.
2. **~~No social layer~~ [RESOLVED → build]** — a class-wide activity feed (`GET /api/activity`, ACTIVITY WORKER in `workers.py`) shows every learner's `AwardGranted`/`QuestCompleted` events live on the Operations Hub. On top of that, a peer-interaction layer now exists: `GET /api/gallery` lists completed submissions across the cohort, and `#/mission/:id/debrief/:userId` lets a learner open a classmate's debrief, see it clearly marked "Viewing X's work" (no Collect button — that's not theirs to collect), and post feedback via `POST /api/timeline/{target_user_id}/{mission_id}/peer-insight`, which publishes `InsightPublished(kind="peer")`. Fixed a real bug along the way: `InsightPublished` was unconditionally marking the "Instructor Review" timeline step complete for any insight, which would have made a classmate's comment falsely look like the teacher's review. Verified end-to-end with a two-user Docker stack and a Playwright session impersonating a second user: gallery lists real submissions, peer comments post and attribute correctly, self-comments are rejected (400), and `teacher_review` correctly stays `active` after a peer comment. **Remaining stretch, not blocking:** reactions/likes (vs. free-text comments only), and no moderation/reporting path if a comment needs to come down.
3. **~~No revise-and-resubmit loop~~ [PARTIALLY RESOLVED → build]** — resubmission is now a first-class, *welcomed* path: a completed mission's brief reframes as "Resubmit — strengthen your work" (explicit copy: first take never punished, nothing earned is taken back), the Vault offers "Strengthen & Resubmit," and a resubmission re-runs the AI review (the award-upgrade path) while correctly **not** re-firing one-time completion effects — which also fixed a real exploit: every resubmission used to re-grant +100 XP and duplicate feed events (verified fixed: XP identical before/after resubmit). **Still open:** a teacher-initiated `RevisionRequested` state ("needs revision" pushed from the review side, visible in the timeline) — today the loop is learner-initiated only.
4. **Graphic-novel content pipeline is undefined [decision + build]** — `UI_SPEC.md` says panels load from `static/content/`, but nothing defines how many chapters exist, who produces the art, what format/resolution, or the chapter↔mission unlock map. The transmedia promise dies without this. Needed: a `chapters.json` manifest schema (panels, captions, unlock conditions), placeholder chapters for all 6 weeks, and an authoring convention the narrative team can fill without engineering help.
5. **~~Collective world-state is missing~~ [RESOLVED → shipped]** — "The Water Rises": every distinct (learner, mission) completion across the whole cohort advances Keel's restoration through 9 named narrative stages (`evoke/world_state.py`, WORLD WORKER in `workers.py`, `GET /api/world-state`). The Hub leads with a Keel Restoration meter; a stage advance publishes `WorldStateAdvanced`, lands in the class feed, pushes live to every open browser (see the live-layer row below), and physically changes the Minecraft world — full-screen `title` announcement to everyone online, challenge-complete sound, totem particles, and a **Restoration Beacon** that gains a glowing layer per stage (anchored near the first player online at first advance, or `KEEL_BEACON_POS`; final stage caps it with a real activated beacon). Verified end-to-end on a live stack: 2 submissions → Stage 1 "First Drops" → feed entry + WS push + RCON celebration + beacon blocks confirmed in-world block-by-block. Stage pacing is a knob (`WORLD_STAGE_STEP`, default 2 for demo; ~roster×12÷8 for a real class) — `world_state.py`'s docstring documents 3 pacing variants (fixed step / roster-aware / milestone-gated) per the Team Wheel convention, with fixed-step shipped.

### AI-augmentable vs. irreducibly human support needs

The AMI-per-cohort model shifts "who operates this" from the school to EVOKE, which makes fleet support a real, ongoing cost — sized to cohort count, not eliminated by architecture. AI compresses *volume* and *latency* on known patterns; it does not remove the need for a human wherever a call is high-stakes, novel, or touches a minor's safety or a legal obligation.

| Support need | What AI can compress | What stays human |
|---|---|---|
| Provisioning | Generating the IaC; once built, spin-up itself can be scripted (automation, not really AI-in-the-loop) | Approving new-cohort provisioning; handling exceptions (custom domain, district SSO/firewall) |
| Monitoring/alerting | First-pass triage against `config/alerts.yml`; auto-remediation of known patterns (restart a crashed container, clear disk) | Novel failures; judgment calls with real stakes (e.g. restoring a corrupted world from backup); staffing on-call hours at all |
| Live-class incident response | A support agent that self-diagnoses and fixes common "won't load" issues in real time | Severe/novel issues, and a fast, clear escalation path when the AI can't fix it — time-sensitive because it's happening during a live 45-minute period |
| Moderation (B1llbot transcripts, Minecraft chat, screenshot uploads) | Classifiers doing first-pass flagging (toxicity, image moderation, jailbreak-attempt detection) — cuts review volume by orders of magnitude | **Cannot be delegated:** anything touching a minor's safety needs human judgment and, in serious cases, carries mandatory-reporting obligations |
| Teacher/customer support | A RAG-based bot over the product's own docs handles most "how do I" tickets instantly | Account-level actions needing authorization (roster fixes, refunds, deletion requests); early customers who want a human relationship while trust is being built |
| Fleet patch rollout | Generating the rollout diff/script; pre-flight checks per instance | The decision to push a change live across running cohorts mid-semester, and any rollback call |
| Offboarding | The runbook itself, scripted and AI-executed given approval | The sign-off to actually delete a district's data — legal/compliance stakes |
